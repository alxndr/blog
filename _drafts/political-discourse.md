American politics relies on candidates and parties behaving in a certain way in response to constituents, press, public discourse, etc. Bald Donny T seems to have broken that (viz the number of things he’s said and done which would have ended any other politician’s career).

Political criticism of a given person’s actions in a given office will identify the target of the criticism usually by naming the person and/or by naming the office.
How does the author choose whether to use the person’s name, or the name of the office, or both?
IMHO the author decides based on the tone of their criticism. If they want to highlight the office perhaps they name the office; if they want to highlight the person perhaps they name the person. 

I believe that Bald Donny T does not feel shame. His brain must have a different circuit where the rest of us have the shame-activation circuit. (Same with empathy.) Rather than shame at being called out, he may in fact react positively to the attention and completely ignore the message. 

Highlighting the person calls attention to their actions, inactions, and speech, and perhaps shame them into changing their behavior. However this won’t work with Trump, because he doesn’t feel shame and instead sees any mention of his name as a positive thing. 

Therefore I am trying to remember to refer to the office, President / POTUS, when criticizing any of the grossly cruel and/or inept actions/messages/policies of his administration. I hope that this highlights the disparity between these depraved actions/messages/policies, and the ideal aspirations of the highest office of America’s government. I hope it also reminds readers that there are political and legal actions we can take in response — that we are not powerless, that we have a collective voice louder than any politician, that the office of President serves at the pleasure of the People. 

It also keeps Bald Donny T’s name out of my mouth. I think that’s the only thing that actually has the power to hurt him.
